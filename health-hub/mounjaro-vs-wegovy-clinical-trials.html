Mounjaro vs Wegovy: What the Clinical Trials Actually Show

The Question Every Patient Asks Us

"My GP mentioned Wegovy. But my friend lost heaps of weight on Mounjaro. Are they the same thing? Should I ask for the other one?"

We hear some version of this question almost every day at both our Chislehurst locations. And it's a genuinely important question — because while Mounjaro (tirzepatide) and Wegovy (semaglutide) are both injectable GLP-1 medications prescribed for weight loss, they are not the same drug. They work differently, cost differently, and suit different patients.

This article breaks down what the clinical trial data actually shows — no marketing spin, just the evidence — so you can have a more informed conversation with your pharmacist about which option might be right for you.

How Each Drug Works

Both medications belong to the incretin-based therapy family, but that's where much of the similarity ends.

Wegovy (Semaglutide 2.4mg)

Wegovy is a GLP-1 receptor agonist — meaning it mimics a single natural hormone called glucagon-like peptide-1. This hormone:

  • Signals satiety to your brain, reducing appetite
  • Slows gastric emptying, so you feel full longer after meals
  • Improves insulin sensitivity and blood sugar regulation

Semaglutide was originally developed as Ozempic for type 2 diabetes. Wegovy uses a higher dose (2.4mg vs 1mg) specifically optimised for weight management.

Mounjaro (Tirzepatide)

Mounjaro is the world's first dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. It does everything Wegovy does, plus activates a second hormonal pathway — glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP). This additional mechanism:

  • Enhances the appetite-suppressing effect of GLP-1
  • Directly reduces fat storage in adipose tissue
  • Improves how the body metabolises nutrients and processes energy

"Think of it this way: Wegovy turns down one appetite dial. Mounjaro turns down two dials simultaneously. Both work — but the dual mechanism tends to produce stronger results."

— Sumeet Banker, Superintendent Pharmacist, REY London

The Clinical Trial Data: Head to Head

Let's look at the landmark trials for each medication. These are the numbers that matter most.

STEP 1 Trial — Wegovy

The STEP 1 trial enrolled 1,961 adults with BMI ≥30 (or ≥27 with comorbidities) and studied semaglutide 2.4mg over 68 weeks.

  • Average weight loss: 14.9% of body weight
  • Percentage losing ≥5%: 86.4% of participants
  • Percentage losing ≥10%: 69.1% of participants
  • Percentage losing ≥20%: 32.0% of participants
20.9%
Mounjaro's average weight loss (15mg dose) In the SURMOUNT-1 trial, the highest dose of tirzepatide produced 20.9% average body weight reduction — approximately 6 percentage points more than Wegovy's STEP 1 result. Sources: SURMOUNT-1 (NEJM 2022), STEP 1 (NEJM 2021)

SURMOUNT-1 Trial — Mounjaro

The SURMOUNT-1 trial enrolled 2,539 adults with similar criteria and studied tirzepatide over 72 weeks.

  • 5mg dose: 15.0% average weight loss
  • 10mg dose: 19.5% average weight loss
  • 15mg dose: 20.9% average weight loss
  • Percentage losing ≥5% (15mg): 91.0% of participants
  • Percentage losing ≥20% (15mg): 57.0% of participants

What Does This Mean in Real Terms?

For a patient weighing 100kg (approximately 15 stone 10 lbs):

  • Wegovy: Average loss of ~14.9kg (2 stone 5 lbs)
  • Mounjaro 15mg: Average loss of ~20.9kg (3 stone 4 lbs)

That's roughly an additional 6kg (nearly 1 stone) of weight loss with Mounjaro — a clinically meaningful difference for most patients.

Side Effects: Are They Different?

Both medications share a similar side-effect profile, primarily gastrointestinal symptoms during the initial weeks as the body adjusts.

Wegovy Side Effects

  • Nausea: ~44% of patients (most common)
  • Diarrhoea: ~30% of patients
  • Vomiting: ~24% of patients
  • Constipation: ~24% of patients

Mounjaro Side Effects

  • Nausea: ~24% of patients
  • Diarrhoea: ~17% of patients
  • Decreased appetite: ~20% of patients
  • Vomiting: ~12% of patients

Interestingly, the rates of nausea and vomiting appear lower with Mounjaro than Wegovy in the respective clinical trials — though direct comparison between separate trials should be interpreted cautiously. Both medications use gradual dose escalation (starting low and increasing every 4 weeks) specifically to minimise GI side effects.

Important Safety Information

Both Mounjaro and Wegovy carry warnings regarding thyroid C-cell tumours observed in animal studies. Neither should be used by patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2 syndrome. Pancreatitis has been reported rarely with both. Always disclose your full medical history during consultation.

Cost & Access in the UK

Availability and cost are practical factors that matter as much as clinical data when choosing a treatment.

NHS Availability

Wegovy has been approved by NICE for NHS use in specialist weight management services, but availability remains patchy. Many areas have significant waiting lists — some patients in South East London report waiting 6–12 months for an NHS assessment.

Mounjaro (as Zepbound for weight management) is newer and currently available primarily through private prescription in the UK. NHS availability for weight loss is expected but not yet widely accessible.

Private Pricing at REY London

  • Wegovy: From £169/month (includes medication, consultation, and ongoing support)
  • Mounjaro: From £189/month (includes medication, consultation, and ongoing support)

Both include face-to-face clinical assessment, prescribing, injection training, and monthly pharmacist check-ins. No GP referral is needed — our pharmacist independent prescribers handle everything in-house.

Which Treatment Suits You?

There's no universal "better" — the right choice depends on your individual circumstances. Here's our general clinical guidance:

Mounjaro May Be Better If:

  • You have a higher BMI (35+) and need maximum weight loss
  • You have type 2 diabetes alongside obesity — the dual mechanism offers superior metabolic benefits
  • You want the strongest clinical evidence for total body weight reduction
  • You've tried other GLP-1 medications without sufficient results

Wegovy May Be Better If:

  • You have a moderately elevated BMI (27–34) and the additional potency isn't necessary
  • You have cardiovascular concerns — semaglutide has robust evidence for CV risk reduction (SELECT trial)
  • You prefer the longer clinical track record — semaglutide has been in use longer with more real-world data
  • You're sensitive to cost and the £20/month difference matters over a long treatment course

"I always tell patients: don't fixate on headlines. The best medication is the one that works for your body, fits your budget, and you'll actually stick with. We'll figure it out together."

— Sumeet Banker, REY London Pharmacy

Can You Switch Between Treatments?

Yes. Switching from Wegovy to Mounjaro (or vice versa) is clinically safe and straightforward when managed properly. Common reasons patients switch include:

  • Inadequate weight loss on the current medication
  • Persistent side effects that haven't improved despite dose adjustment
  • Supply issues — Wegovy has experienced periodic stock shortages in the UK
  • Cost considerations — switching to Wegovy to save on monthly costs

Our pharmacists manage the transition carefully, typically allowing a brief washout period and starting the new medication at the lowest dose with gradual escalation.

The Bottom Line

Both Mounjaro and Wegovy represent the most significant advance in weight management in decades. The data is clear: these are not fad treatments — they are clinically proven, evidence-based medications that produce meaningful, sustained weight loss for the majority of patients.

On raw weight-loss numbers, Mounjaro has the edge. On cardiovascular data, Wegovy is ahead. On cost, Wegovy is marginally cheaper. On tolerability, the data is broadly similar with a slight advantage to Mounjaro.

The most important step isn't choosing between them — it's having the conversation with a qualified prescriber who can assess your individual situation and recommend the right option for you.
