What Is Mounjaro?

If you've been struggling with weight loss despite trying every diet and exercise plan in the book, you're not alone. Thousands of patients across South East London — from Chislehurst to Bromley, Orpington to Bexley — have found themselves in the same frustrating cycle.

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) represents a genuine breakthrough. Unlike older weight-loss medications that target a single mechanism, Mounjaro is the world's first dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist — meaning it works on two hormonal pathways simultaneously to regulate appetite, blood sugar, and metabolism.

Approved by the MHRA and available through pharmacist independent prescribers like our team at REY London, Mounjaro has quickly become the most talked-about treatment in modern weight management.

The Clinical Evidence: What the Numbers Actually Show

We believe in transparency — so let's look at exactly what the landmark clinical trials found. The SURMOUNT-1 study enrolled over 2,500 adults with obesity or overweight with at least one weight-related condition.

SURMOUNT-1 Results at a Glance

  • 5mg dose: Average weight loss of 15.0% of body weight
  • 10mg dose: Average weight loss of 19.5% of body weight
  • 15mg dose: Average weight loss of 20.9% of body weight
  • Placebo group: Average weight loss of 3.1% of body weight

To put this in perspective: for a patient weighing 100kg (approximately 15 stone 10 lbs), the highest dose typically results in losing around 21kg — that's over 3 stone.

Why Patients Wish They'd Started Sooner

We hear the same thing from patients almost every week: "I wish I'd done this months ago." Here's what they typically report:

Beyond the Scale

Weight loss is just the beginning. Our patients consistently describe improvements across multiple dimensions of their health:

  1. Energy levels: Many patients report feeling more energetic within the first 4–6 weeks, even before significant weight loss occurs.
  2. Joint pain: Reduced load on knees, hips, and lower back translates to noticeably less pain — especially for patients with osteoarthritis.
  3. Blood pressure: Average systolic BP reductions of 6–8 mmHg observed in clinical trials.
  4. Blood sugar control: Mounjaro was originally developed for type 2 diabetes — HbA1c improvements are often dramatic.
  5. Sleep quality: Patients with obstructive sleep apnoea frequently report improved sleep and reduced snoring.
  6. Mental health: Reduced anxiety around food choices and improved body confidence.

How Mounjaro Actually Works in Your Body

Understanding the science helps explain why Mounjaro is so effective compared to earlier treatments.

Traditional GLP-1 medications (like semaglutide/Wegovy) target one receptor. Mounjaro targets two:

The Dual-Action Mechanism

GLP-1 receptor activation: Reduces appetite, slows gastric emptying (you feel full longer), and improves insulin sensitivity.

GIP receptor activation: Enhances the GLP-1 effect, directly reduces fat storage, and improves how your body metabolises nutrients. This second pathway is what makes Mounjaro unique — and explains the superior weight-loss results compared to GLP-1-only drugs.

Important Safety Information

Mounjaro carries a boxed warning regarding thyroid C-cell tumours observed in animal studies. It is contraindicated in patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2). Always disclose your full medical history during your consultation.

Side Effects: What to Expect

Like all medications, Mounjaro can cause side effects. The good news is that most are mild, temporary, and manageable with proper guidance.

Common Side Effects (First 2–4 Weeks)

  • Nausea — The most common side effect, affecting around 24% of patients. Usually mild and improves as your body adjusts.
  • Decreased appetite — This is actually the desired therapeutic effect — a sign the medication is working.
  • Diarrhoea — Affects approximately 17% of patients. Staying hydrated and eating small, frequent meals helps.
  • Constipation — Less common but can occur. Increased fibre and water intake usually resolves this.
  • Injection site reactions — Minor redness or itching at the injection site, typically resolving within hours.

Our pharmacists guide you through a gradual dose-escalation protocol specifically designed to minimise these effects. We start at the lowest dose (2.5mg) and increase every 4 weeks.

Am I Eligible for Mounjaro?

Eligibility is assessed during a face-to-face consultation with one of our pharmacist independent prescribers. General criteria include:

  • BMI of 30 or above (classified as obese)
  • BMI of 27 or above with at least one weight-related health condition (type 2 diabetes, hypertension, high cholesterol, sleep apnoea)
  • Age 18 or over
  • No contraindications (such as MEN2, medullary thyroid carcinoma history, or pancreatitis)

You do not need a GP referral. Our pharmacists are qualified independent prescribers who can assess, prescribe, and provide ongoing care directly.

Common questions about Mounjaro from our South East London patients

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist that works by mimicking two natural hormones involved in appetite regulation and blood sugar control. It reduces hunger, slows gastric emptying, and helps your body use insulin more effectively — leading to significant, sustained weight loss.

In the SURMOUNT-1 clinical trial, participants on the highest dose of Mounjaro lost an average of 20.9% of their body weight over 72 weeks. For someone weighing 100kg, that's approximately 21kg. Individual results vary based on starting weight, diet, exercise, and adherence to the treatment plan.

You may be eligible if you have a BMI of 30 or above, or a BMI of 27+ with a weight-related health condition such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, or high cholesterol. Our pharmacists conduct a thorough clinical assessment to determine suitability.

The most common side effects are gastrointestinal — nausea, diarrhoea, and decreased appetite — particularly during the first few weeks as your body adjusts. These typically improve with time and can be managed by slowly titrating the dose upward under pharmacist supervision.
